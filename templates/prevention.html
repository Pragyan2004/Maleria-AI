Malaria Prevention & Safety Tips

Important Note

These prevention tips are for informational purposes. Always consult with healthcare professionals for personalized medical advice.

Prevention Methods

Mosquito Nets

Use insecticide-treated mosquito nets (ITNs) while sleeping. Nets should be properly tucked under mattresses.

Indoor Spraying

Use indoor residual spraying (IRS) with insecticides approved by WHO for malaria control.

Protective Clothing

Wear long-sleeved shirts, long pants, and socks in malaria-prone areas, especially during dusk and dawn.

Personal Protection

Insect Repellents

Apply EPA-registered insect repellents containing DEET, picaridin, IR3535, or oil of lemon eucalyptus.

Antimalarial Drugs

Take prescribed antimalarial medication before, during, and after travel to high-risk areas.

Environmental Control

Eliminate standing water, use window screens, and maintain clean surroundings to reduce mosquito breeding.

Travel Safety Tips

Pre-Travel Planning
  • Consult doctor 4-6 weeks before travel
  • Get recommended vaccinations
  • Research malaria risk in destination
During Travel
  • Take antimalarials as prescribed
  • Use mosquito nets in accommodations
  • Avoid outdoor activities at dusk/dawn
Post-Travel
  • Continue medication as directed
  • Monitor for symptoms for 1 year
  • Seek medical care if symptoms appear

Know the Symptoms

Common Symptoms
  • Fever and chills
  • Headache
  • Fatigue and body aches
  • Nausea and vomiting
Severe Symptoms (Emergency)
  • Confusion or seizures
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Dark or bloody urine
  • Persistent vomiting
Emergency: Seek immediate medical attention if severe symptoms appear. Malaria can be fatal if not treated promptly.

High-Risk Areas

Africa

Sub-Saharan Africa accounts for approximately 94% of malaria cases and deaths worldwide.

South Asia

India, Bangladesh, and other South Asian countries have significant malaria transmission.

Latin America

Parts of Central and South America, especially Amazon basin regions.

Check before you travel: Visit CDC Malaria Information by Country for updated travel advisories.
